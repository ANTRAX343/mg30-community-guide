## Cabinet Characteristics

### **Guitar Cabinet Types**

#### **4x12 Cabinets**
- **Power & Presence**: Maximum volume and stage presence
- **Full Frequency Response**: Complete low-end to high-end spectrum
- **Best For**: Live performance, heavy rock, metal

#### **2x12 Cabinets**
- **Balanced Response**: Good compromise of power and portability
- **Versatile**: Works well for most genres
- **Best For**: Studio recording, medium venues

#### **1x12 Cabinets**
- **Focused Sound**: Tight, direct response
- **Studio Friendly**: Easy to mic and control
- **Best For**: Home recording, practice, smaller venues

### **Bass Cabinet Types**

#### **8x10 Configurations**
- **Maximum Low-End**: Deep, powerful bass response
- **Classic Rock Sound**: The sound of classic rock and punk
- **Best For**: Large venues, vintage bass tones

#### **4x10 Configurations**
- **Modern Punch**: Clear, punchy bass with good midrange
- **Versatile**: Works across many genres
- **Best For**: Modern rock, funk, pop

#### **Celestion Speakers**
- **G12T-75**: Modern, aggressive tone with tight low-end
- **Greenback**: Vintage, warm tone with musical compression

#### **Eminence Speakers**
- **Swamp Thang**: American-voiced with strong midrange

- **Legend**: Classic American rock tone

## IR Technology

### **What are Impulse Responses?**
IRs capture the acoustic signature of real speaker cabinets and microphone combinations, providing authentic cabinet simulation without the physical cabinet.

### **MG30 IR Features**
- **1024 Sample Resolution**: High-quality IR processing
- **Third-Party IR Support**: Load custom IRs via NUX Quick Tone
- **Real-Time Processing**: No latency in IR processing

### **Choosing the Right IR**

#### **For Recording**
- **1x12 cabinets**: Focused, easy to mix
- **Close mic IRs**: Detailed, present sound

#### **For Live Performance**
- **4x12 cabinets**: Maximum presence and power
- **Room mic IRs**: Natural, spacious sound

#### **For Practice**
- **2x12 cabinets**: Balanced, not overwhelming
- **Neutral IRs**: Good starting point

## Documentation Format

Each cabinet includes:
- **Description** - Cabinet characteristics and real-world reference
- **Configuration** - Speaker count, size, and arrangement
- **Frequency Response** - Tonal characteristics and frequency emphasis
- **Best Applications** - Recommended uses and musical styles
- **Microphone Placement** - How the IR was captured
